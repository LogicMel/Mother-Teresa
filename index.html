Life & Brief History

Saint Mother Teresa commonly known as Mother Teresa was a Roman Catholic Religious Sister and missionary of Albanian orgin who lived for most of her life in India. Mother Teresa was born on August 26, 1910 in Skopje, Macedonia.

Mother Teresa went to India in 1928, where she joined a religious community, The Irish Order of Sisters of Loretto. Initially she taught at a convent school in Kolkata, taking her final vows in 1937, She became the school's principal.

In 1948, deeply conscious of the proverty all around her in kolkata, she felt a irresistible call to help the poor. She left the convent to work alone in the slums.She opened her House for the Dying in 1952; it was a shelter for the homeless, offering them a final refuge to save them fro the indiginit of dying on the streets.

Mother Teresa founded the Missionaries of Charity, a Roman Catholic religious congregation, in 1950, which in 2012 consisted over 4,500 Sisters and is active in 133 contries. She and her order became directly responsible to the Pope in 1956.

Mother Teresa was the recipient of nemerous honours inculding the 1979 Nobel Peace Prize. Following a prolonged illness, Mother Teresa passed away on September 5, 1997. In 2003, she was beatified as "Blessed Teresa of Calcutta". She was canonised (recognised by the church as a Saint Teresa) on 4th September 2016, and the aniversary of her death, September 5, was made her feast day.
